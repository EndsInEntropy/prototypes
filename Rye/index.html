▶ How it's worked out
Dough temperature is the average of its four contributors, plus any heat from the mixer. Rearranged to solve for the water: water = (target × 4) − (levain + flour + room + friction) This dough runs warm on purpose — 28°C speeds up its very short fermentation. Keep water below 43°C so you don't harm the levain.

Defaults are set to the 90% rye recipe. Only the target dough temp is fixed by the recipe — levain, flour and room are yours to measure.

The night before
1
Prepare the levain9:00 pm

Mix, cover, and leave 12 hours at 23–24°C until risen, bubbly and smelling of dark red wine.

272g whole rye flour
272g water
41g ripe starter (100% hydration)
The next day
2
Mix9:00 am

Warm the mixing water so the dough finishes at 28°C — check the Calculator. Combine everything with a dough whisk or wet hands until no dry flour remains. Don't knead; rye needs none. Gather into a ball and take its temperature.

272g whole rye flour
60g high-protein white bread flour
272g water (hold a little back)
11g fine sea salt
all the levain
3
Bulk ferment9:15–9:35 am · 20 min

Cover and rest just 20 minutes at 23–24°C. No stretch-and-folds. If your dough came out below 28°C, give it a little longer.

4
Shape9:35 am

Heavily dust an 8-inch banneton with rye. Flour the surface, the dough top and your hands. Scrape out floured-side down. Fold left, top, right, then bottom into the middle; flip and round into a rough ball. Set seam-down in the basket, dust the top with rye, cover.

5
Proof9:45–10:45 am · 1 hr

Proof 1 hour at room temperature. Meanwhile preheat the oven with a stone or steel to 230°C (450°F).

6
Bake10:45 am

Ready when puffy with cracks ~1cm wide across the top. Tip onto parchment seam-down, load, and steam the oven. Bake 30 min at 230°C, then drop to 205°C (400°F) and bake 30–35 min more until deep brown and hollow-sounding (200–204°C internal). Cool 30 min, then rest wrapped 24 hours before slicing.

Total dough 1,200g · 90% hydration · 45% prefermented flour
